<p>

<h2>About Me</h2>

<p>
I am a senior undergraduate in Theoretical Physics (Class of 2022) at Lanzhou University,
advised by Associate Prof. Jiaqi Dong. My research focuses on
<strong>nonequilibrium statistical physics</strong> and <strong>stochastic thermodynamics</strong>,
covering linear response theory, fluctuation theorems, thermodynamic uncertainty relations (TUR),
and programmable magnetic-field control of potential landscapes.
</p>

<p>Thesis: <strong><em>Fluctuation-Response Behavior of Non-equilibrium Brownian Motor</em></strong> &nbsp;·&nbsp; Defense: April 2026</p>

<h2>Research Interests</h2>

<div class="research-grid" style="display:grid;grid-template-columns:repeat(auto-fit,minmax(220px,1fr));gap:1em;margin:1em 0 1.5em;">
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>Stochastic Thermodynamics</h3>
    <p style="font-size:0.88em;color:#6b7280;margin:0">Defining work, heat, and entropy production along individual trajectories; studying probability distributions and time-reversal symmetries far from equilibrium.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>Linear Response Theory</h3>
    <p style="font-size:0.88em;color:#6b7280;margin:0">Verifying the FDT validity boundary in nonequilibrium steady states; characterizing the effective range of linear response for an underdamped Brownian rotor.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>Fluctuation Theorems & TUR</h3>
    <p style="font-size:0.88em;color:#6b7280;margin:0">Numerically verifying consistency of entropy production definitions; quantifying the inertial correction to the TUR bound (Q ≈ 0.80 vs. 1.00).</p>
  </div>
  <div class="research-card">
    <h3>Magnetic Field Control</h3>
    <p style="font-size:0.88em;color:#6b7280;margin:0">Designing a programmable magnetic trap using an electromagnet array for optimal transport, information erasure, and Brownian Carnot engines.</p>
  </div>
</div>
